The flow

Three approvals. Zero confusion.

Every swap goes through the same predictable path. Both workers agree, you confirm, the schedule updates itself.

1

Worker A proposes a swap

Picks a shift to give up, picks a coworker's shift to take instead. Two taps, one request.

2

Worker B accepts (or declines)

Gets a push notification, taps Accept or Decline. If declined, the request closes — you never see it.

3

Manager approves

You see only the swaps that both workers already agreed to. One tap to confirm. Schedule updates instantly for everyone.

Outcomes

What changes the moment you ship this to your team

No more after-hours messages

Workers handle swaps among themselves. Only confirmed trades come to you, and only during the day.

No more "I never agreed to that"

Every swap requires both workers to tap Accept. Written record. Disputes drop to zero.

Schedule stays in sync

Approved swaps update the published schedule automatically. Workers see the new version instantly.

Coverage stays balanced

Swaps don't break role minimums or rest-hour rules — the system blocks trades that would create a violation.

Workers feel ownership

Giving your team a structured way to solve their own scheduling problems beats them venting at you.

You stay in control

Nothing changes without your final approval. Configurable: you can require boss approval, or delegate to a shift manager.
