Where each tool wins

Be honest with yourself about which problem you have

"I just need a digital roster"

If you're happy building schedules manually and just want them visible to your team, both tools work. Kloki is cheaper. Choose Kloki if cost is the only factor.

"I want the schedule done for me"

The auto-scheduler is Wrok's biggest difference. Most tools at this price point (including Kloki) require you to drag and drop shifts manually. Wrok generates them.

"Workers swap shifts constantly"

If your team trades shifts often, you need a real workflow with mutual approval and manager confirmation. Wrok has it; Kloki and most other Croatian tools don't.

"I run multiple locations"

Both tools support multi-location. Wrok adds the Shift Manager role with configurable per-location permissions, which is meaningful when you delegate.

"My accountant needs night/Sunday hours"

Honest answer: Kloki is ahead here today. We're building payroll-ready categorized export. If this is your #1 need this quarter, Kloki may fit better right now.

"I want a tool I won't outgrow"

Wrok scales from 4 to 100+ workers across multiple locations with role-based permissions and configurable rules. Most small-tool competitors top out around 30 workers.
