Wrok vs When I Work vs Deputy: a simple, affordable shift scheduling app

If you are comparing shift scheduling tools for a small or mid-sized team, the honest question is not which app has the longest feature list. It is which app your managers and staff will actually use every week. Wrok, When I Work, and Deputy all schedule shifts. This page lays out how they differ so you can pick the right fit, with Wrok positioned as the simple, affordable, automation-first option.

Choosing a scheduling app for a small shift team

When I Work and Deputy are well-known, mature platforms with broad feature sets and, in Deputy's case, a strong footprint in larger and enterprise operations. They are good products. But for a cafe, bar, restaurant, retail shop, clinic, or warehouse with a handful of staff, all that breadth can mean more setup, more training, and per-user pricing that climbs as you add people.

Wrok takes the opposite approach. It is built for small and mid-sized teams that want to stop building schedules by hand without learning a heavy platform. You add your team, set your shifts, and generate a full week with one tap. Pricing is flat, so your bill does not grow every time you hire someone.

When Wrok is the better choice

Wrok is likely the best fit if most of the following are true for your team:

  • You run a small or mid-sized shift team, from a handful of staff up to about 100 workers.
  • You want a full weekly schedule generated automatically instead of building it by hand in a spreadsheet.
  • You want predictable, flat pricing and do not want your bill to rise every time you add a worker.
  • You want to start free and only pay when you grow. Wrok is free forever for up to 6 workers.
  • You want your team up and running in minutes, without training sessions or a long onboarding.
  • You need the core essentials done well: automatic scheduling, shift swaps, time off, hours tracking, and team chat, all in one place.
  • You are on a budget and want a genuinely affordable When I Work or Deputy alternative for a small business.

When you might pick the others

Being honest builds trust, so here is where When I Work or Deputy may serve you better. No single tool is right for everyone.

  • You run a large or enterprise operation with many locations and complex compliance needs. Deputy in particular is built with larger organizations in mind.
  • You need deep, specialized integrations with specific payroll, POS, or HR systems that a given competitor supports out of the box.
  • You require advanced labor-law, wage-rule, or auditing features that go beyond core scheduling.
  • Your team is already trained on and comfortable with an existing platform, and switching cost outweighs the benefit right now.

If those describe you, evaluate When I Work and Deputy directly and check their current plans. If you are a smaller team that mostly needs scheduling done quickly and affordably, Wrok is worth a look first.
